IELTS Writing Tips

Task 1 (Academic: Graphs, Charts, Maps)

  • Understand the Data: Identify trends, comparisons, and key features.
  • Use Structure:
    • Introduction: Paraphrase the question.
    • Overview: Summarize the main trends.
    • Body Paragraphs: Explain details with comparisons.
  • Avoid Opinions: Just describe the data objectively.
  • Use a Variety of Sentence Structures: Comparisons, trends, and passive voice help.

Task 1 (General: Letter Writing)

  • Formal, Semi-Formal, or Informal? Choose based on the situation.
  • Structure Matters:
    • Opening: Greet appropriately.
    • Body: Address all bullet points.
    • Closing: Use a suitable sign-off.
  • Use Polite & Natural Language: Ensure your tone matches the context.

Task 2 (Essay Writing)

  • Plan Before Writing: Spend 5 minutes brainstorming ideas.
  • Structure:
    • Introduction: Paraphrase the topic + give your opinion (if needed).
    • Body Paragraphs: One idea per paragraph with examples.
    • Conclusion: Summarize key points.
  • Use Linking Words: "However," "On the other hand," "For instance," etc.
  • Avoid Repetition: Use synonyms and varied sentence structures.

General Writing Tips

  • Word Count: 150 (Task 1), 250 (Task 2)—don’t write too much or too little.
  • Grammar & Vocabulary Matter: Use a mix of sentence types and avoid basic words.
  • Practice Timed Writing: 20 minutes (Task 1), 40 minutes (Task 2).
